# triage — what's worth my next 25 minutes

Three lenses, scored fast (high / med / low — don't overthink). Apply each to every item:

- **Value** — does this actually matter? (real outcome, not just *feels* productive)
- **Urgency** — does it have to happen now? (a hard deadline, or just my anxiety?)
- **Energy** — do I have what this needs right now? (deep focus / quick reply / nothing left)

## Route each item — one of four, with a one-line *why*
- **Do now** — high-value AND (urgent OR energy-matched). Open the tab, do it.
- **Schedule** — high-value, low-urgency, or wrong energy for now. Block a real time on a real day.
- **Delegate** — someone else can do it well enough, faster, or it's not actually your call.
- **Drop** — low-value, low-urgency, or the cost of doing it exceeds the cost of dropping it. Say no, archive, close.

## Anti-pattern
Triaging without a deliverable — re-scanning the same list, sorting it three different ways, feeling busy. **Every triage pass produces a routed list.** If you finish without a route assigned per item, you didn't triage; you procrastinated.

**Output:** one bullet per item — `route — item — one-line why`. Then do the "do now" pile.
