# Outbound & Positioning

Reach the right people with messages that read like a peer wrote them, and sharpen the positioning those messages express.

## Principles
1. **Write like a peer, not a vendor.** Lead with their world ("you"), not yours ("we/I"). If you can delete the opener and the email still makes sense, the personalization is fake.
2. **One low-friction ask.** Every sentence earns its place; one clear, easy next step.
3. **Positioning precedes copy.** If the message isn't landing, the positioning is usually the problem, not the wording.
4. **Sequences beat single sends.** Most replies come from follow-ups; cadence and persistence matter more than any single line.
5. **Non-hype voice.** No buzzwords, no "just checking in," no AI tells. (Chykalophia voice; see `conversion-copywriting/references/voice-and-headlines.md`.)

## Framework pickers
- **Cold email** (`references/cold-email-and-cadence.md`): PAS (default), BAB (transformation), QVC (brevity for execs), 3 C's (Compliment, Case study, CTA — the agency-outreach default), SCQ (consultative). Plus subject-line and cadence rules with benchmarks.
- **Qualification & discovery** (`references/positioning-and-methodologies.md`): SPIN (consultative discovery), Challenger (reframe with insight), MEDDIC/MEDDPICC (complex-deal scorecard), BANT (fast triage), JTBD (sell the outcome).
- **Positioning & messaging** (`references/positioning-and-methodologies.md`): April Dunford's five components (competitive alternatives → unique attributes → value → target customers → market category) and the Value Proposition Canvas (jobs/pains/gains ↔ products/pain-relievers/gain-creators, tested for "fit").

## Workflow
1. Clarify the audience, the trigger/relevance, and the single desired action.
2. For outreach: pick the framework, draft 1 short email + a 4–5 step cadence, each touch carrying one new value point (never "just checking in").
3. For qualification: choose the methodology to deal complexity; produce the question set or scorecard.
4. For positioning: run Dunford's five components and pressure-test with the Value Proposition Canvas; express the result as a one-line positioning statement and a value prop.

## Guardrails
No fabricated personalization, fake mutual connections, or invented "case studies." Respect anti-spam norms; honor a breakup email if you send one. Keep claims true and specific.
