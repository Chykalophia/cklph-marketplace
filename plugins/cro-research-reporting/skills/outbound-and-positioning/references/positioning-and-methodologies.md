# Positioning, Value Props & Sales Methodologies

## Positioning — April Dunford's five components (derive in order)
1. **Competitive alternatives** — what the customer would use if you didn't exist (often a spreadsheet or manual process, not a named rival).
2. **Unique attributes** — what you have that the alternatives lack.
3. **Value** — the benefit those attributes deliver (translate features to value).
4. **Target customers** — who cares most about that value.
5. **Market category** — the context that makes your value obvious to those customers.
Express as a one-line positioning statement, then validate with the canvas below. Best for clients who are mis-categorized or "sound like everyone else."

## Value Proposition Canvas (Strategyzer) — test for "fit"
- Customer profile: **Jobs** (functional/social/emotional), **Pains**, **Gains**.
- Value map: **Products & services**, **Pain relievers**, **Gain creators**.
- Fit = every important job/pain/gain on the right is matched by a specific element on the left. Use it to prove messaging maps to what the buyer actually wants.

## Above-the-fold / message-market fit
Headline (the promise) + subhead (the mechanism) + primary CTA + proof bar. The promise must match the source (ad/keyword/email) that drove the visit. Mismatch is the top landing-page leak.

## B2B sales methodologies (use to deal complexity)
- **SPIN** — Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-payoff. A questioning sequence for consultative discovery: establish the situation, surface problems, make implications vivid, let the buyer articulate the value of solving.
- **Challenger** — Teach, Tailor, Take control. Reframe how the prospect thinks (lead with insight), tailor to the stakeholder, assertively guide the deal. For reshaping demand, not just answering it.
- **MEDDIC** — Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, Champion. A qualification scorecard for complex deals.
- **MEDDPICC** — MEDDIC + Paper process (procurement/legal) + Competition. For enterprise.
- **BANT** — Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline. Fast triage; weak for complex deals.
- **JTBD (Jobs-to-be-Done)** — people "hire" a product to get a job done; sell the outcome, not the features. Frames discovery and positioning around the job.

## When to reach for which
- Early discovery: SPIN + JTBD.
- Crowded/commoditized perception: Challenger + Dunford positioning.
- Complex/enterprise deal hygiene: MEDDIC/MEDDPICC.
- Quick inbound triage: BANT.
