# Cold Email Frameworks & Cadence

## Frameworks (pick to the prospect)
- **PAS** — Problem, Agitate, Solution + soft CTA. Default; for problem-aware prospects.
- **BAB** — Before, After, Bridge. For transformation offers.
- **QVC** — Question, Value, CTA. Brevity for C-suite.
- **AIDA** — Attention (a stat/hook), Interest, Desire (proof), Action. Data-driven, higher-ticket.
- **3 C's** (Compliment, Case study, CTA) — the default for agency/services outreach.
- **SCQ** — Situation, Complication, Question. Consultative; mirrors how people present to leadership.
- **Star-Story-Solution** — a customer character, their challenge, the result. Humanizes the pitch.
- **PASTOR** — for multi-email sequences (one element per touch).

## Principles (encode as rules)
- Lead with their world; "you" beats "we/I."
- Personalization must connect to the problem, not just name-drop their company.
- One low-friction ask. Short. Scannable on a phone.
- Subject lines: 2 to 4 words, lowercase, internal-looking, no pitch.
- Banned AI tells: "I hope this email finds you well," "I came across your profile/company," "leverage," "synergy," "best-in-class," "circle back," "just checking in."

## Follow-up cadence (with benchmarks — directional)
- ~55% of replies come from follow-ups; roughly half of senders never follow up once.
- 4 to 7 touch campaigns see materially higher reply rates than 1 to 3. Cap around 4 follow-ups (5 total).
- Cadence: Day 0, +3, +7, +14, +21 to 28. Widen the gap each step.
- Best days Tuesday to Thursday; best times mid-morning or early afternoon, prospect-local. Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons.
- One new value point per email. Never "just checking in" (it lowers booking rates).
- A breakup email ("reply 1/2/3" or a clean close) recovers ~10 to 15%. If you send it, honor it.

Source landscape: coreyhaines31/marketingskills cold-email references (MIT); benchmarks are directional aggregates, present as ranges, not guarantees.
