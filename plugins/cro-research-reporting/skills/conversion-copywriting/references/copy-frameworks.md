# Copywriting Frameworks

Public methods (paraphrased, not copied from any author's prose). Each: what it is + when to use.

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
Hook attention, build interest with relevance, create desire with proof/outcome, then a clear CTA. Use for ads, long landing pages, full-page arcs.

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution
Name the pain, amplify the cost of leaving it unsolved, present the solution + soft CTA. The workhorse for problem-aware readers. Use for PDPs with a clear pain, ads, cold-email openers.

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge
Painful current state, the ideal future state, your product as the bridge. Use for transformation offers and emotional decisions.

## FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits
Feature (what it is) to Advantage (what it does) to Benefit (what it means for them). Discipline: never stop at a feature. Use for product pages, spec-heavy items, comparison blocks.

## 4 Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push
Promise the outcome, help them Picture it, give Proof, then Push to act. Use for short landing pages, email bodies, offer sections.

## PASTOR — Problem, Amplify, Story, Testimony, Offer, Response
Problem, Amplify the cost, Story of someone who solved it, Testimony/proof, Offer, Response (the ask). The best long-form structure for consulting, education, and complex B2B services; each element can be its own email in a sequence.

## StoryBrand SB7 — customer as hero, brand as guide
1. A Character (customer) wants something. 2. Has a Problem (external, internal, philosophical). 3. Meets a Guide (your brand) with empathy + authority. 4. Who gives a Plan. 5. And Calls them to Action (direct + transitional). 6. That helps avoid Failure (stakes). 7. Ending in Success (vivid resolved life). Use for homepage and brand-level messaging.

## Which to use where
| Format | Body framework | Headline |
|---|---|---|
| PDP / product page | PAS or FAB | 4 U's |
| Landing page (one offer) | BAB or 4 Ps (AIDA for full arc) | 4 U's |
| Email | PAS hook; 4 Ps for offers | 4 U's subject |
| Ad | PAS or AIDA (brief) | 4 U's |
| Homepage / brand | StoryBrand SB7 | 4 U's |
| Long-form sales / consulting | PASTOR | 4 U's |

Worked micro-example (PAS, a premium sit-stand desk):
- P: "By 3pm your back aches and your focus is gone."
- A: "Another year at the wrong desk means more lost afternoons and a cheap fix you already regret."
- S: "A desk that rises to you in one tap, assembled in 15 minutes, with a 30-day trial. Stand easy."
