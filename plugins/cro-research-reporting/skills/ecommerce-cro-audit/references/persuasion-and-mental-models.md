# Persuasion Principles & Conversion Mental Models

Use ethically: deploy scarcity/urgency only when genuine; never fabricate social proof or counters.

## Cialdini's 7 principles of persuasion
1. **Reciprocity** — give first (free value, tools, generous trials); people return favors.
2. **Commitment & Consistency** — secure a small yes first; people stay consistent with prior actions.
3. **Social Proof** — people do what they see others do (review counts, logos, "bestseller," UGC).
4. **Authority** — credentials, expert/press endorsements, "featured in."
5. **Liking** — we say yes to those we like and resemble (relatable founder story, shared language).
6. **Scarcity** — genuine limited availability raises perceived value (real low-stock, time-limited, exclusive).
7. **Unity** — shared identity, the "one of us" frame (insider language, shared values).

## Conversion mental models (map each to a gate)
- **Anchoring** — the first number framed sets the reference (show compare-at / "from" / premium tier first). → Desire/price.
- **Decoy effect** — a third, deliberately inferior option makes the target option look obviously right. → tier/pricing pages.
- **Framing** — "95% lean" beats "5% fat"; "cost per use," "X/day." → value/price.
- **Loss aversion / Prospect theory** — losses loom larger than gains; frame what they miss (free returns removes loss risk). → De-risk.
- **Zeigarnik / open loops** — incomplete tasks nag; progress bars, "1 step left," abandoned-cart reminders. → Act/retention.
- **Goal-gradient** — motivation rises near the goal; show progress toward free shipping / completion. → Act/AOV.
- **Default effect** — most people accept the default; ship a complete, attractive default config/selection. → Act.
- **Paradox of choice** — too many options paralyze; reduce, default, and guide. → Act.
- **Peak-end rule** — people judge an experience by its peak and end; nail the unboxing/confirmation and the "wow" moment. → retention.

## Pricing psychology
- Charm vs rounded pricing (test, don't assume; luxury often prefers rounded).
- Rule of 100 (percentage vs absolute discounts depending on price).
- Good-better-best with a highlighted recommended tier (decoy/anchor).
- Mental accounting / "$X per day" or "per wear" framing — but for luxury use value-multiplication, not cheapness.

## Quick reference: challenge → models to reach for
| Problem | Reach for |
|---|---|
| Low add-to-cart | Social proof, default config, anchoring, clearer value framing |
| Price objections | Anchoring, decoy/tiering, framing, loss aversion, value-multiplication |
| Building trust | Authority, social proof, unity, risk-reversal (guarantee/returns) |
| Need urgency (genuine) | Scarcity, Zeigarnik, goal-gradient — only when real |
| Retention/repeat | Reciprocity, peak-end, commitment, goal-gradient |
