# Beyond Ecommerce — landing pages, lead-gen & SaaS signup

The same diagnostic (locate the leak → five gates → high-impact levers → impact/effort) transfers to any conversion surface. What changes is the "conversion event" and a few surface-specific levers.

## Map the five gates to non-ecommerce
- **Comprehend** → message match + clarity: the page must instantly say what it is and who it's for, matching the ad/email that drove the click. Mismatch is the #1 landing-page leak.
- **Desire** → outcome/benefit-led hero, not feature-led; show the product/result (screenshots, demo, before/after).
- **Trust** → logos, testimonials/case studies with names + numbers, ratings, security/compliance badges, named proof.
- **De-risk** → free trial / money-back / "no credit card", clear pricing, data-privacy reassurance.
- **Act** → one primary CTA above the fold, repeated; minimal form friction.

## Landing pages
- One page, one goal, one primary CTA. Remove site nav that leaks attention.
- Above-the-fold: headline (the promise) → subhead (the how) → hero visual → primary CTA → one trust cue.
- Match the source: the headline should echo the ad/keyword that brought the visitor.
- Speed and mobile thumb-zone CTAs matter as much as in ecommerce.

## Lead-gen forms
- **Every field costs conversion.** Ask only what's needed now; defer the rest to later steps or enrichment.
- Multi-step > one long form for higher-commitment asks (progress + momentum).
- Inline validation, clear labels, sensible defaults, and a benefit-framed button ("Get my free audit", not "Submit").
- State the value exchange and what happens next ("No spam. We reply within 1 business day.").

## SaaS signup / activation
- Reduce time-to-value: minimize signup fields, offer SSO, defer setup, show a populated/demo state rather than an empty one.
- "No credit card required" for trials lifts top-of-funnel; gate the card at the value moment.
- Pricing page: anchor tiers, highlight the recommended plan (accent, "most popular"), make the value-per-tier scannable; surface annual savings and a guarantee.
- Onboarding: a guided first action (the "aha"), not a blank dashboard — the SaaS analog of a styled default config.

## What stays identical
- Ground in real funnel/analytics; benchmark against the right category; conversion is multiplicative across gates; prioritize by impact × effort; show recommendations as an annotated blueprint; never use fake urgency; measure on clean traffic.
