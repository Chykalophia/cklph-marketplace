# weekly-sync — 90 percent solve, 10 percent status

The meeting is a discipline. Same day, same time, same agenda, same length — every week. If you skip a week, you skipped the week.

## Target — 90 minutes, fixed agenda

| Block | Time | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| Segue | 1 min | One personal/professional win or loss from the week. Round the room. Move on. |
| Scorecard review | 5 min | Walk the numbers. Off-target two weeks running → drop into issues. See `scorecard`. |
| Quarterly priority status | 5 min | Each priority: on track / off track / done. One word. Off track → issues. See `rocks`. |
| Customer / employee headlines | 3 min | Anything in either bucket the team needs to know. Headlines, not stories. |
| **Issues block** | **60-75 min** | **The real meeting.** Identify, prioritize, solve. See `issues-ids`. |
| To-do recap | 5 min | Who owns what, by when. Read it back. Write it down. |
| Meeting rating | 1 min | Each person calls a 1-10. Anything below 8 — ask why next week. |

## The issues block is the meeting

Status takes 10 minutes because status is wrap-up. Solving is the work. If your meeting is 60 minutes of status and 30 minutes of solving, you have an update channel, not a leadership meeting. Invert it.

Issues get routed through `issues-ids` — Identify, Discuss, Solve. Don't free-form.

## End with two things — always

- **To-dos** — specific (verb + noun + deadline), one owner each, read back to the room.
- **Rating** — every attendee, 1-10, out loud. The rating is a leading metric for the meeting itself. Three sub-8 weeks in a row = the meeting is broken; fix it.

## Anti-patterns

- **Status-update theater** — each person reads what they did. Cancel it; ask for written updates instead.
- **No agenda** — meeting becomes whatever the loudest person brought.
- **No issues block** — you're not solving anything; you're just synchronizing.
- **Ending without to-dos** — nothing moves between now and next week.
- **Ending without a rating** — no feedback loop on the meeting itself.
- **Scope creep on time** — 90 minutes means 90 minutes. If you can't finish the issues, the prioritization was wrong.

## Triggers

Setting up a recurring leadership meeting. Fixing a meeting that isn't moving the business. "Our standups are status theater." "We talk about everything and decide nothing."

Pairs with `scorecard` (the numbers block), `rocks` (the priority block), and `issues-ids` (the loop inside the issues block).
